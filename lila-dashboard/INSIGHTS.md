# LILA Atlas - Level Design Insights

Based on the visualization and analysis of the 5-day gameplay data, here are three key insights for the Level Design team.

### 1. Map Popularity Discrepancy
**Insight**: Players heavily favor **Ambrose Valley** over other maps.
**Evidence**: Across the 796 matches analyzed, 567 matches (71%) took place on Ambrose Valley, 171 on Lockdown (21%), and only 59 on Grand Rift (8%).
**Actionable Items**: 
- Determine if Ambrose Valley is the default selected map in the UI. If so, a "Random Map" feature or better UI visibility for Grand Rift could balance queues.
- Analyze player feedback on Grand Rift to see if the layout or loot distribution discourages selection. Metrics to track: Map Selection Rate and Matchmaking Queue Times.

### 2. High Density of "Hot Drops" in Ambrose Valley
**Insight**: A significant portion of player kills (1,799 kills total on Ambrose Valley) occur in highly concentrated zones rather than being spread out evenly across the map.
**Evidence**: Viewing the heatmap overlay for Kills and Traffic on Ambrose Valley reveals distinct "hot zones" where players frequently engage.
**Actionable Items**: 
- If Level Designers want a more spread-out early game, high-tier loot spawns should be decentralized away from these major hubs.
- If high engagement is desired, ensure these hot zones have sufficient cover and multiple entry/exit points to prevent severe spawn camping. Metrics to track: Average Time to First Fight, Loot Distribution Density.

### 3. Storm Deaths are Extremely Low
**Insight**: The one-directional storm is rarely the direct cause of player deaths.
**Evidence**: Out of thousands of combat deaths, there were only 17 Storm Deaths on Ambrose Valley and 17 on Lockdown.
**Actionable Items**:
- This suggests players are highly aware of the storm and move early, or the storm's pace is too forgiving.
- If the design goal is for the storm to be a more lethal threat or force tighter engagements, consider increasing the storm's shrink speed or damage output.
- Conversely, if the storm is meant to simply shepherd players rather than kill them, the current tuning is perfect. Metrics to track: Death by Storm %, Average Distance from Storm.
